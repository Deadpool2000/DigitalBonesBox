Humerus - Detailed Information

What is the Humerus?

The humerus is the long bone of the upper arm or forelimb that extends from the shoulder to the elbow. It is crucial for the movement and function of the arm and serves as the attachment point for several muscles.

Function

The humerus allows for a wide range of arm movements including lifting, rotating, and swinging. It also supports the upper arm and contributes to the structure of the shoulder and elbow joints.

Key Features

  • Head
  • Greater Tubercle
  • Lesser Tubercle
  • Intertubercular Groove
  • Deltoid Tuberosity
  • Capitulum
  • Trochlea
  • Olecranon Fossa
  • Coronoid Fossa
