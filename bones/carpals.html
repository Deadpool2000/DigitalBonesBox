    What are Carpals?

    The carpals are the eight small bones that make up the wrist (or carpus) that connects the hand to the forearm. These bones are arranged in two rows, forming part of the structure that allows for movement of the wrist.

    Function

    The primary function of the carpals is to facilitate the range of motion in the wrist and provide support for the hand.
